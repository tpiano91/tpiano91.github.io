About Me

I’m a Research Analyst at the Ontario Arts Council, a public agency that supports arts organizations across Ontario through grants and funding. I also work at the Royal Conservatory of Music as an examiner, collaborative pianist, and chamber music coach at the Taylor Academy (in addition to maintaining a small private piano studio and regularly serving as an adjudicator for local music festivals/competitions). These roles give me a unique perspective on the arts ecosystem - from both the funding and institutional sides—which continues to shape how I think about data, access, and impact in the sector.

My path into data analytics developed naturally through my work as a musician and academic, particularly during my doctoral research in collaborative piano pedagogy. Engaging with research and large-scale projects led me to explore how data can inform decision-making and tell meaningful stories.

Today, I approach analytics with a focus on clarity, structure, and communication - skills deeply rooted in my musical training and professional experience. Aside from arts funding, I’m interested in how data intersects with other fields, including sports, urban infrastructure, and language. Whether it’s analyzing trends in tennis performance or understanding what makes cities more livable, I’m drawn to how data helps explain complex systems in intuitive ways.

When I'm not working, practicing piano, or playing concerts (or attending concerts), you'll likely find me watching travel vlogs (brainstorming ideas for my next trip/adventure), watching tennis, cooking, at the gym, practicing my French (badly), or exploring my amazing city, Toronto.
